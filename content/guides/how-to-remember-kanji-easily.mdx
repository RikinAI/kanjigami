If you're learning Japanese, you've probably heard someone say:

> "Kanji is hard."

Honestly?

Kanji isn't hard.

Trying to memorize 2,000 random symbols with no system is hard.

Today, I'm going to show you the **5-Kanji-a-Day Method** I used to learn over **1,300 kanji**, build vocabulary at the same time, and improve reading speed — all in less than an hour per day.



## Why Most Beginners Struggle With Kanji

Most beginners make the same mistake.

They get excited and try to memorize:

- 20 kanji
- 30 kanji
- Sometimes even 50 kanji in a day

Three days later?

They forget almost everything.

The problem isn't effort.

The problem is trying to learn too much before your brain has time to absorb it.



## Step 1: Target Only 5 Kanji Per Day

Instead, focus on just **5 kanji per day**.

Spend around **5 minutes** on each kanji.

For every kanji, learn:

- Meaning
- Reading
- Example words
- One example sentence

That's it.

Twenty-five minutes of focused learning is far more effective than two hours of cramming.



## Step 2: Use Memory Tricks

Your brain loves stories.

It hates random information.

That's why mnemonic techniques are one of the most effective ways to learn Japanese kanji.

**Example**

Kanji for small,little **小**

**Memory trick**: Imagine a firework was supposed to explode into the sky. Instead, it only produced three tiny sparks.

Now every time you see 小, you'll think "small spark."

It sounds silly.

Good.

The sillier the memory, the longer it sticks.

You can create your own memory tricks or You can try using the **Memory Trick** section on KanjiGami during the early stages.

Don't try to be a hero and memorize everything through brute force.

Let your brain do what it's naturally designed to do.



## Step 3: Learn Words, Not Just Kanji

A lot of learners memorize a kanji and stop there.

Big mistake.

Kanji only become useful when you see them inside words.

For every kanji, learn around **5 useful words**.

Example: **日** (Sun / Day)

**日**本 = Nihon - Japan 

**日**曜**日** = Nichiyoubi - Sunday

毎**日** = Mainichi - Every Day

休**日** = Kyuujitsu - Holiday

**日**記 = Nikki - Daily Journal 

Now you're not learning one thing.

You're learning six things at once.

- One kanji
- Five vocabulary words



## Step 4: Read Japanese for 10 Minutes Every Day

This is where the magic happens.

After learning your five kanji, spend just **10 minutes reading**.

Not a textbook.

Not grammar drills.

Read actual Japanese content at your level.

Some useful websites for beginners:
-[Watanoc](https://watanoc.com/)
-[NHK News Easy](https://news.web.nhk/news/easy/)
-[Nippon Talk](https://www.nippontalk.com/en)

### What To Do When You Forget a Kanji

When you encounter a kanji and can't remember it:

1. Stop reading.
2. Go back to KanjiGami.
3. Check the meaning.
4. Check the reading.
5. Continue reading.

I call this:

> Instant Memory Repair

Instead of forgetting and reviewing later, you fix the gap immediately.

Your brain learns:

> "This kanji matters. I'd better remember it next time."



## Why This Method Works

This method combines three powerful learning principles:

1. Small daily goals
2. Memory tricks
3. Reading in context

You're not just memorizing kanji.

You're using kanji.

And that's the difference.

## Final Thoughts

If you've been struggling with kanji, stop trying to memorize hundreds at once.

Use the **5-Kanji-a-Day Method**.

Every day:

✅ Learn 5 Kanji  
✅ Spend 5 Minutes on Each  
✅ Learn 5 Useful Words  
✅ Read for 10 Minutes  

Do that consistently, and six months from now you'll be amazed at how many kanji you recognize without even thinking about it.

Remember:

> Kanji isn't a sprint.
>
> It's a daily habit.

Five kanji today is better than fifty kanji you'll forget tomorrow.
